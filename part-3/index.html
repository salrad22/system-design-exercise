The Challenge

Design the Notification System

Omar placed his order from Pizza Roma. Now he's watching the app, waiting for updates. Here's what needs to happen:

  1. "Your order has been received by Pizza Roma"
  2. "Khalid is on the way to pick up your order"
  3. "Khalid has picked up your order"
  4. "Khalid is 5 minutes away" (real-time tracking)
  5. "Your order has been delivered! Rate your experience"

Q1: Which of these notifications should be pushed to Omar in real-time vs. available when he refreshes? What protocol would you use for real-time updates?

Q2: The driver's location updates every 3 seconds. Should every update be stored in PostgreSQL? Where would you store it instead, and why?

Q3: If the notification service is down for 2 minutes during a traffic spike, should Omar miss those updates entirely? How would you ensure he eventually sees them?

Q4: Pizza Roma receives orders from 50 different customers during dinner rush. Should each notification be a direct API call from Yalla Delivery's server, or is there a better pattern?

There is no single right answer. Think in trade-offs. Design your architecture, take a screenshot, and share your solution with #YallaSDChallenge.

We'll explore this scenario in Part 4 when we cover communication protocols and real-time systems.
